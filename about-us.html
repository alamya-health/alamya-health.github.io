The Team

Our mission is to create a world where every individual has access to the highest quality genomic information and technologies, enabling them to live healthier, longer, and more fulfilling lives.

We envision a future where personalized medicine is the standard of care, and where genomics guides the prevention, diagnosis, and treatment of disease.

Leadership

Kimberly Hood, JD, MPH

EXECUTIVE

Kimberly Hood is an attorney and public health professional.

Her work focuses on ensuring equitable access to quality healthcare and on defining the social determinants of health.

She led Louisiana’s Office of Public Health and was responsible for overseeing the creation and implementation of the nation’s first subscription-based drug pricing model, and has interfaced with the US healthcare payer system to enact policy around DNA sequencing in the neonatal intensive care unit.

Cornelius Boerkoel, MD, PhD

PRESIDENT

Cornelius Boerkoel is a practicing medical geneticist and a physician scientist.

He has extensive experience defining processes underpinning precision and genomic medicine practice as well as delineating genetic causes of disease and disease mechanisms.

Through leadership roles at the Rare Disease Foundation, the Undiagnosed Disease Program at the NIH, and the Sanford Imagenetics Research Center, he has facilitated the adoption of genomic medicine at scale.

Scientific

Steven Jones, PhD

GENOMICS LEAD

Steven Jones is a world-leading expert in genomics and bioinformatics.

As a director of the BC Genome Sciences Centre, he has pioneered the development of genomic technologies for over a decade to diagnose genomic diseases and gain insight into the structure and function of the genome.

His recent work focuses on exploring utility of long-read sequencing technology on the Nanopore platform in the applied clinical research setting.

Rosanna Weksberg, MD, PhD

EPIGENETICS LEAD

Rosanna Weksberg is a practicing medical geneticist and a physician scientist.

She is internationally recognized for her work in the discovery of DNA methylation changes underpinning or indicative of human health and disease.

Her recent work focuses on applying such DNA methylation changes as biomarkers of disease and of therapeutic efficacy.

Tudor Oprea, MD, PhD

DATA INTEGRATION LEAD

Tudor Oprea is a researcher with experience in biochemistry and machine learning.

He has extensive experience in identifying molecular and biochemical targets for therapeutic development with the use of advanced machine learning approaches.

His recent work focuses on complex data integration from biochemical and biomedical data sources using artificial intelligence.

Cheryl Shuman, MSc, CGC

EDUCATION AND GENETIC COUNSELING LEAD

Cheryl Shuman practiced as clinical genetic counselor and as an educational and administrative leader.

She was also involved in clinical research primarily related to the (epi)genetic disorder, Beckwith-Wiedemann syndrome and to the provision and practice of genetic counseling.

Cheryl retired from clinical and program administrative roles in 2020 and currently serves on several advisory boards.

Medical

Lorne Clarke, MD

MEDICAL LEAD

Lorne Clarke is a medical geneticist and clinician scientist.

He has worldwide recognition for his role in delineating the disease mechanisms of underlying lysosomal storage diseases and in the development of enzyme replacement and chaperone therapies.

He is currently professor emeritus in the department of medical genetics at the University of British Columbia and focuses on advising philanthropic organizations and biotechnology corporations on rare disease therapeutics.

Hui-Lin Chin, MBBS, MMed

MEDICAL LEAD (ASIA)

Hui-Lin Chin is a practicing pediatrician and medical geneticist specializing in genomic medicine.

Her expertise is in the diagnosis and longitudinal care of individuals with genetic disorders, and she has facilitated the implementation of genomic medicine practice in various medical disciplines within Singapore.

She also teaches precision medicine at undergraduate and various postgraduate levels. Her research interests are in rare diseases, genomic analysis and clinical variant interpretation.

Stephanie Huynh, MSc, CGC

GENETIC COUNSELING LEAD

Stephanie Huynh is a clinical genetic counselor.

She has several years of experience in the interpretation and counseling of the association of genomic variation with human disease.

Her recent work focuses on the delineation of the processes underlying the practice of genomic medicine and on the use of software tools for the education and consenting of individuals to genomic testing.

Miao He, PhD

BIOCHEMICAL LEAD

Miao He is a clinical biochemical geneticist and researcher.

She has wide-ranging experience in directing clinical laboratories and in clinical test design as well as in the discovery of the genetic and mechanistic bases of human biochemical disease.

Her recent work focuses on discovery of congenital disorders of glycosylation and delineation of biomarkers of disease and therapeutic efficacy.
