Leadership

Kimberly Hood, JD, MPH

Executive

Kimberly Hood is an attorney and public health professional.

Her work focuses on ensuring equitable access to quality healthcare and on defining the social determinants of health.

She led Louisiana’s Office of Public Health and was responsible for overseeing the creation and implementation of the nation’s first subscription-based drug pricing model, and has interfaced with the US healthcare payer system to enact policy around DNA sequencing in the neonatal intensive care unit.

Cornelius Boerkoel, MD, PhD

President

Cornelius Boerkoel is a practicing medical geneticist and a physician scientist.

He has extensive experience defining processes underpinning precision and genomic medicine practice as well as delineating genetic causes of disease and disease mechanisms.

Through leadership roles at the Rare Disease Foundation, the Undiagnosed Disease Program at the NIH, and the Sanford Imagenetics Research Center, he has facilitated the adoption of genomic medicine at scale.

Business

Tom Dolan

Revenue Lead

Tom Dolan is a business leader within the life science and medical fields.

He has extensive sales, marketing, and leadership experience in software and complementary products and services.

His recent work focuses on healthcare educational resources that enable informed decisions, accurate coding, and regulatory compliance.

Michael Raymond, JD, CPA

Finance Lead

Michael Raymond is an attorney and a Certified Public Accountant.

He has over 30 years of experience in multinational corporate finance including accounting, audit, financial planning & analysis, taxation, and treasury functions.

His recent work in the field of rare disease is to lead the Snyder-Robinson Foundation, where he is a founder and current executive director, seeking support and answers for individuals affected by Snyder-Robinson disease including his son.

Alireza Baradaran, MD, PhD

Operations Lead

Alireza Baradaran is a medical geneticist and physician scientist.

He has considerable experience in the application of molecular biology and genomics to the diagnosis and characterization of genetic diseases.

His recent work focuses on therapeutic discovery for precision therapies.

Michael Mboob, MBA

Personnel Lead

Michael Mboob is a business leader and healthcare strategist.

He has extensive experience managing teams to define and achieve deliverables efficiently and effectively within financial constraints.

His recent focus is leading operations of regional micro-hospitals.

Meriam Waqas

Projects Lead

Meriam Waqas is a research management professional.

She has successfully interfaced with clinicians, researchers, and patients on research projects that focus on delivery of cutting-edge genomics research to families affected by rare genetic disease.

Her recent work focused on supporting the Care4Rare Canadian Rare Disease project, and the BC Children's Rare Disease Discovery Hub.

Medical

Lorne Clarke, MD

Medical Lead

Lorne Clarke is a medical geneticist and physician scientist.

He has worldwide recognition for his role in delineating the disease mechanisms of lysosomal storage diseases and in the development of enzyme replacement and chaperone therapies.

His recent work focuses on advising philanthropic organizations and biotechnology corporations on rare disease therapeutics.

Laura Li, MD, PhD

Laboratory Operations Lead

Laura Li is a clinical geneticist, genomics researcher, and entrepreneur.

She has experience in creating and maintaining a CLIA/CAP certified genomics laboratory operations.

Her recent work focuses on using machine learning tools to assist with the clinical interpretation of genomic variation.

Stephanie Huynh, CGC

Genetic Counseling Lead

Stephanie Huynh is a clinical genetic counselor and a geneticist.

She has several years of experience in the interpretation and counseling of the association of genomic variation with human disease.

Her recent work focuses on the delineation of the processes underlying the practice of genomic medicine and on the use of software tools for the education and consenting of individuals to genomic testing.

Miao He, PhD

Biochemical Lead

Miao He is a clinical biochemical geneticist and researcher.

She has wide-ranging experience in directing clinical laboratories and in clinical test design as well as in the discovery of the genetic and mechanistic bases of human biochemical disease.

Her recent work focuses on discovery of congenital disorders of glycosylation and delineation of biomarkers of disease and therapeutic efficacy.

Katherine Dixon, PhD

Medical Reporting Lead

Katie Dixon is a molecular human genetics researcher.

She is at the forefront of utilizing long-read sequencing data on the Nanopore platform for the diagnosis of human genetic disease.

Her recent work focuses on applying long-read sequencing to the molecular diagnosis of congenital disorders and cancer predisposition syndromes.

Wenjie Lu, PhD

Pharmacogenetics Lead

Wenjie Lu is a leader in clinical pharmacology, particularly pharmacogenetics.

She has played significant roles in the development of clinical guidelines for pharmacokinetic and pharmacodynamic genotypes.

Her recent work focuses on therapeutic development and clinical trials.

Bobbi Andera

Regulatory Lead

Bobbi Andera is a laboratory compliance officer and scientist.

She has extensive understanding of laboratory procedures, regulations, billing practices, and reporting practices.

Her current work focuses on improving laboratory business processes and compliance.

Scientific

Steven Jones, PhD

Genomics Lead

Steven Jones is a world-leading expert in genomics and bioinformatics.

As director of the BC Genome Sciences Centre, he has pioneered the development of genomic technologies for over a decade to diagnose genomic diseases and gain insight into the structure and function of the genome.

His recent work focuses on exploring utility of long-read sequencing technology on the Nanopore platform in the applied clinical research setting.

Rosanna Weksberg, PhD, MD

Epigenetics Lead

She is internationally recognized for her work in the discovery of DNA methylation changes underpinning or indicative of human health and disease.

Melissa Haendel, PhD

She focuses on ways to connect healthcare systems, basic science research, and patients; through development of data integration technologies, innovative communication strategies, and collaborative education and outreach.

Technical
